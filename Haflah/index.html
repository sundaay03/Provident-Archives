Provident Graduation

is a traditional Islamic year-end celebration commonly held by educational institutions such as Pesantren (Islamic boarding schools) or Madrasah. Derived from Arabic, the term literally means "end-of-year party," serving as a ceremonial graduation or thanksgiving event to mark the completion of an academic period. The event typically features a blend of religious rituals—such as Khatmil Qur'an (completing the recitation of the Quran)—and cultural performances, providing a platform for students to showcase their talents to teachers, parents, and the local community.
